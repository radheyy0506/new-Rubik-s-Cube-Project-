SHADOW CUBE

Master Level: 6x6

🟩 STEP 1: Massive Centers

On a 6x6, each center is a 4x4 block (16 pieces per face!).

Solve White & Yellow first to establish your axis.
Build centers row by row (1x4 bars).
Precision is key: One wrong slice can scramble two other faces.

🟦 STEP 2: Quad-Edge Pairing

Each edge has 4 small pieces. We pair them in sets to reduce them to a single unit.

Use Inner Slice Moves (Reduction Logic)

Strategy: Pair them into 2-piece sets first, then merge those into the final 4-piece edge.

🟨 STEP 3: 3x3 Reduction Stage

The cube is now a "Mega-3x3". Use your standard Layer-by-Layer algorithms.

Solve Cross & F2L.
Treat the 4x4 centers and quad-edges as single pieces.

STEP 4: 6x6 Parity Fix

Parity on 6x6 is very common due to the even number of layers. Use these precise algorithms:

OLL Parity (Flipped Edge)

r2 B2 U2 l U2 r' U2 l2

PLL Parity (Edge Swapping)

2R2 U2 2R2 Uw2 2R2 Uw2

“For big cubes like 5×5 and 6×6, we use the Layer-by-Layer method in three main stages: solve centers, pair edges, then solve the cube like a 3×3. Parity errors may occur, but they can be fixed using special algorithms. This method is simple, logical, and helps us systematically solve even the largest cubes.”
