5x5 Advanced Reduction

🧩 STEP 1: 3x3 Center Blocks

On a 5x5, each face has 9 center pieces. You must solve them in a 3x3 grid.

  • Solve White and Yellow first.
  • Build the centers row by row (1x3 bars).
  • Remember the color order: White opposite Yellow, Blue opposite Green, Red opposite Orange.
Pro Tip: Don't panic if you scramble a solved center while working on another. Use "Slice-Turn-Slice Back" moves.

🟦 STEP 2: Triple Edge Pairing

Each edge consists of 3 pieces. Pair the outer wings with the central edge piece.

Basic Pairing Algorithm:

(Uu) R U' R' U' F' U F

📌 Repeat this logic until all 12 "Triple Edges" are complete.

🟨 STEP 3: 3x3 Stage

The cube is now reduced! Treat the 3x3 centers and the triple-edges as single units.

  • Solve the Cross.
  • Solve the first two layers (F2L).
  • Orient and Permute the last layer (OLL/PLL).

STEP 4: 5x5 Parity

5x5 parity is similar to 4x4 but involves the middle slice of the triple edge.

OLL Parity (Flipped Center Edge)

r U2 l' U2 r' U2 l

PLL Parity (Edge Swapping)

2R2 U2 2R2 Uw2 2R2 Uw2

SUCCESS! 🎉

Once reduced to a 3x3, the 5x5 is just a game of patience. Take it slow, pair those edges, and you'll have it solved in no time.
